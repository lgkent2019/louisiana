Cajun Music

Cajun music is most easily recognized by three elements: the button accordion, the fiddle and the French language. Most Cajun songs are either two-steps or waltzes and are written to accompany particular dance steps. It incorporates influences from Irish, German, African, Native American and Appalachian traditions. Some performers include Lawrence Walker, the Lafayette Playboys and D.L. Menard.

Zydeco Music

Zydeco is a style of music from southwest Louisiana that draws from Black American blues, Louisiana French Creole, and Native American musical culture. Zydeco music uses accordions as well as French lyrics like Cajun music. Though the style differes in that Cajun music is closer to country music and Zydeco music is closer to blues. Some Zydeco performers are Clifton Chenier, Buckwheat Zydeco and Rockin' Dopsie.
